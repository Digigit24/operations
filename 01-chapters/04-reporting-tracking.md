# CHAPTER 4 — REPORTING & TRACKING
### Client Reports, Delivery Tracking, Risk Surfacing

🔵 ATTACH-TO-CLAUDE — First draft. Edit freely.

> Help PMs produce reports that show real value, track delivery against scope, and catch delivery risks early — before the client does.

---

## What we track per client

1. **Scope** — what was promised in the contract
2. **Delivered** — what's been completed
3. **In-progress** — current week's work
4. **Blocked** — and why
5. **Health score** — 🟢 / 🟡 / 🔴
6. **Wins** — outcomes, not activity
7. **Risks** — things heading the wrong way

---

## Where tracking lives

All in Notion:

- **`clients` DB** — scope, contract terms, status
- **`tasks` DB** — delivery progress
- **`client-state.md`** — narrative + mood
- **`/08-reports/`** per client — formal reports archive

---

## Weekly report format (Sprint clients)

Sent every Friday or Saturday morning. Under 200 words. Skim-able in 60 seconds.

```
1. What we did this week (3-5 bullets, specific)
2. Wins to celebrate (1-2 — outcomes, not activity)
3. What's coming next week (clear, scoped)
4. What we need from you (specific asks, or "Nothing — we're on track")
5. One strategic note from your account team (the human touch)
```

The 5th bullet is what differentiates us. It's where the PM's voice shows up. Never AI-feeling.

---

## Monthly report format (Retainer clients)

Sent first Monday of the month. One page or a 2–3 min Loom video.

```
1. Snapshot dashboard (5 numbers: traffic, leads, content output, SEO moves, GMB metrics)
2. What we accomplished last month (5-7 bullets)
3. Insights from the data (2-3 things we learned, NOT just numbers)
4. This month's focus (3 priorities)
5. Strategic recommendation (1 thing worth doing differently)
```

The bar: every report should give the client at least one thing to think about, not just things we did.

---

## 90-Day Final Report (Sprint)

The wow document. Sent on Day 87–88, before the renewal pitch.

```
1. Where you started (Day 1 snapshot)
2. Where you are now (Day 90 snapshot — same metrics)
3. What we built together (assets, systems, foundations)
4. What's working (with proof)
5. What's next (the retainer pitch, naturally)
```

Make it visual. Make it premium. This is the document that converts.

---

## Delivery vs scope check

Every Monday, the Command Center compares:

- What's in scope (from contract)
- What's been delivered (from tasks log)
- What's behind schedule

Flag the PM if anything is behind by **>7 days**.

---

## Risk surfacing

Watch for:

- Two missed deadlines on the same client
- Client mood drop in last 2 interactions
- Health score downgrade
- Stale client-state (no update in 7+ days)
- A skill that ran with errors
- Client gone quiet (no contact in 10+ days)
- Compliance flag from any content QA

Any of these → flag immediately, suggest a recovery plan.

---

## Client check-in prep

Before any client call, produce a 1-page brief:

1. Last 5 interactions summary
2. What's open / blocked
3. Likely questions the client will ask
4. The ONE strategic message to land in the call
5. The ONE ask the PM should make

PM reads in 5 minutes. Walks in prepared.

---

## What to surface to the PM weekly (not the client)

Internal weekly summary the Command Center delivers via the Saturday Wrap routine:

- Wins worth celebrating across all clients
- Risks flagged this week
- Stale states (anything >7 days old)
- Tasks slipping (anything >7 days behind)
- Insights observed across clients this week (candidates for the central insights file)

---

## Reporting anti-patterns

- Don't pad reports with activity (clients see through it)
- Don't use vague metrics ("engagement is up") — give numbers or skip it
- Don't bury bad news — surface it with a recovery plan
- Don't write reports that read like generic AI output — the PM's voice must show through
- Don't send reports on holidays (move to next business day)

---

## Quality bar for client-facing reports

Every report must:

- Be specific (numbers, names, dates)
- Tie work to client goals (not our deliverables)
- Show one insight, not just one activity
- End with something forward-looking
- Be skim-able in under 90 seconds
